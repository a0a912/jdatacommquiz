Q1. Which of the following data communication characteristics refers to the variation in packet arrival time, which can negatively impact the quality of streaming video or audio?
   A. Timeliness
   B. Accuracy
   C. Jitter
   D. Delivery

   Answer: C
   Explanation: Jitter is the term for uneven delay in the delivery of data packets, such as when video packets sent at a constant rate arrive at irregular intervals.

Q2. In data communications, what is the role of a protocol?
   A. It is the physical path through which a message travels.
   B. It is the information or data that is being communicated.
   C. It represents a set of rules that governs the communication between devices.
   D. It is the device that sends the data message.

   Answer: C
   Explanation: A protocol acts as an agreement between devices, defining the rules for formatting, timing, and error control to ensure they can understand each other.

Q3. A communication channel where both stations can transmit and receive data, but not at the same time, is operating in which mode?
   A. Simplex
   B. Full-duplex
   C. Multiplex
   D. Half-duplex

   Answer: D
   Explanation: This mode allows two-way communication, but devices must take turns transmitting, similar to how walkie-talkies operate.

Q4. What is a primary disadvantage of a mesh network topology?
   A. The entire network fails if a single central device goes down.
   B. It is difficult to isolate faults because all devices share a single cable.
   C. The amount of cabling and the number of I/O ports required can be prohibitively expensive.
   D. Unidirectional traffic means a single break in the link can disable the entire network.

   Answer: C
   Explanation: Because every device has a dedicated point-to-point link to every other device, the installation complexity and hardware costs are very high.

Q5. In which network topology does all communication pass through a central controller, such as a hub or a switch?
   A. Bus
   B. Ring
   C. Mesh
   D. Star

   Answer: D
   Explanation: The star topology is defined by its central connection point; devices do not communicate directly with each other but relay messages through the central hub.

Q6. What is the key difference between a LAN (Local Area Network) and a WAN (Wide Area Network)?
   A. A LAN uses packet-switching, while a WAN uses circuit-switching.
   B. A LAN connects hosts, is typically privately owned, and covers a limited geographical area, whereas a WAN interconnects networks over a large area and is often leased.
   C. A LAN must use a bus topology, while a WAN must use a mesh topology.
   D. A LAN is only for personal use, while a WAN is for business use.

   Answer: B
   Explanation: These distinctions in size, ownership, and the types of devices connected (hosts vs. connecting devices) are the primary criteria for distinguishing between LANs and WANs.

Q7. How many point-to-point links are needed for a fully connected mesh topology with 8 nodes?
   A. 56
   B. 28
   C. 16
   D. 8

   Answer: B
   Explanation: Using the formula `$n(n-1)/2$`, for 8 nodes the calculation is `$8(7)/2 = 28$`.

Q8. Which of the following describes a key characteristic of a packet-switched network?
   A. It establishes a dedicated, continuous circuit between two end systems for the duration of the communication.
   B. It is only efficient when working at its full capacity.
   C. Data is transmitted in discrete blocks, which can be stored and forwarded by routers.
   D. It guarantees that data packets will arrive without any delay.

   Answer: C
   Explanation: Packet-switched networks break data into packets that are routed independently, allowing for more efficient use of the network's capacity by sharing it among multiple communications.

Q9. The precursor to the Internet, developed in the late 1960s by the Department of Defense's ARPA, was known as:
   A. NSFNET
   B. CSNET
   C. ARPANET
   D. MILNET

   Answer: C
   Explanation: The Advanced Research Projects Agency Network (ARPANET) was the first packet-switched network and the foundation of the modern Internet.

Q10. What was the key innovation Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn introduced in their 'Internetting Project' that allowed dissimilar networks to be linked?
   A. The Interface Message Processor (IMP)
   B. The Network Control Protocol (NCP)
   C. The concept of a gateway to serve as an intermediary device
   D. The development of the World Wide Web

   Answer: C
   Explanation: They devised the idea of a gateway (now called a router) to handle the complexities of transferring data between networks with different packet sizes, interfaces, and transmission rates.

Q11. The TCP/IP protocol suite was created by splitting the original Transmission Control Protocol (TCP). What was the primary responsibility assigned to the Internet Protocol (IP) part?
   A. Handling higher-level functions like segmentation and reassembly of data.
   B. Ensuring the physical transmission of bits over the wire.
   C. Handling datagram routing.
   D. Managing user applications like file transfer and email.

   Answer: C
   Explanation: The split was designed so IP would be responsible for the core task of routing packets across the internetwork from source to destination.

Q12. What is the status of a Request for Comment (RFC) that is stable, well-understood, and has been implemented successfully by at least two independent groups?
   A. Proposed Standard
   B. Internet Standard
   C. Experimental
   D. Draft Standard

   Answer: D
   Explanation: A specification is elevated from Proposed Standard to Draft Standard after it has been shown to be stable and has at least two independent and interoperable implementations.

Q13. Which Internet administration body is a forum of working groups focused on long-term research topics related to Internet protocols, applications, and architecture?
   A. Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF)
   B. Internet Architecture Board (IAB)
   C. Internet Society (ISOC)
   D. Internet Research Task Force (IRTF)

   Answer: D
   Explanation: The IRTF is explicitly focused on long-term research, as opposed to the IETF's focus on current engineering and standards issues.

Q14. A keyboard and a monitor are classic examples of devices operating in which data flow mode?
   A. Simplex
   B. Half-duplex
   C. Full-duplex
   D. Point-to-point

   Answer: A
   Explanation: Communication is unidirectional: the keyboard only sends data to the computer, and the monitor only receives data from the computer.

Q15. What is a significant advantage of a bus topology in terms of installation?
   A. It is very robust because of redundant paths.
   B. It uses less cabling than mesh or star topologies.
   C. Adding new devices is very easy and does not disrupt the network.
   D. It provides enhanced security because messages are sent on dedicated lines.

   Answer: B
   Explanation: A single backbone cable runs the length of the network, and devices connect to it with shorter drop lines, minimizing the total cable length.

Q16. What are backbones and provider networks collectively known as in the context of the Internet's structure?
   A. Customer Networks
   B. Peering Points
   C. Internet Service Providers (ISPs)
   D. Internetworks

   Answer: C
   Explanation: Backbones are often called international ISPs, and provider networks are called national or regional ISPs; together they form the infrastructure that provides Internet service.

Q17. In a ring topology, how is a signal passed from a sender to a non-adjacent destination device?
   A. The signal is broadcast to all devices simultaneously, and only the destination accepts it.
   B. A central controller forwards the signal directly to the destination.
   C. The signal is passed from device to device along the ring until it reaches its destination.
   D. The sender establishes a dedicated point-to-point link directly to the destination.

   Answer: C
   Explanation: Each device in the ring incorporates a repeater that regenerates the signal and passes it to the next device in the sequence.

Q18. Which technology for accessing the Internet typically allows for simultaneous voice and data communication over the same telephone line?
   A. Dial-up service
   B. Cable network service
   C. DSL Service
   D. Point-to-point WAN

   Answer: C
   Explanation: Digital Subscriber Line (DSL) technology operates at higher frequencies on the telephone line than voice calls, allowing both to be used at the same time.

Q19. An RFC labeled as 'Required' indicates that it...
   A. is useful, but not necessary for minimum conformance.
   B. describes work related to an experimental situation.
   C. must be implemented by all Internet systems to achieve minimum conformance.
   D. is significant from a historical perspective but has been superseded.

   Answer: C
   Explanation: Protocols like IP and ICMP are labeled as 'Required' because they are fundamental to the operation of the Internet.

Q20. How are numerical data typically represented in data communications?
   A. Using a character code like Unicode or ASCII.
   B. By directly converting the number to its binary equivalent.
   C. As a matrix of pixels representing the shape of the digits.
   D. As a continuous analog signal.

   Answer: B
   Explanation: Numbers are represented by bit patterns corresponding to their binary form to simplify and speed up mathematical operations by the computer.

Q21. What is the primary function of a tap in a bus topology?
   A. To terminate the signal at the end of the cable to prevent reflection.
   B. To regenerate and boost the signal as it passes along the cable.
   C. To act as a central controller for all devices on the network.
   D. To connect a device's drop line to the main backbone cable.

   Answer: D
   Explanation: A tap is a physical connector that either splices into or punctures the main cable, creating a connection point for a node.

Q22. When two or more networks are connected, they form an...
   A. internetwork or internet.
   B. intranet.
   C. topology.
   D. protocol suite.

   Answer: A
   Explanation: An internet (lowercase i) is the generic term for any collection of interconnected networks.

Q23. What is meant by 'real-time transmission' in the context of timeliness?
   A. The data is delivered to every device on the network at the exact same moment.
   B. The system delivers data without any errors.
   C. Data is delivered as it is produced, in order, and without significant delay.
   D. The system can store data and forward it at a later, more convenient time.

   Answer: C
   Explanation: This is crucial for applications like streaming video and audio, where data must be processed as it arrives to provide a smooth user experience.

Q24. The development that led to an 'explosion of Internet applications' in the 1990s by adding commercial applications was the invention of the...
   A. TCP/IP protocol suite.
   B. ARPANET.
   C. World Wide Web (WWW).
   D. Peer-to-peer applications.

   Answer: C
   Explanation: Invented by Tim Berners-Lee, the Web made the Internet accessible and useful to a non-technical audience, leading to massive commercial and popular growth.

Q25. Which type of connection involves more than two specific devices sharing a single link?
   A. Point-to-point
   B. Simplex
   C. Multipoint
   D. Duplex

   Answer: C
   Explanation: Also called multidrop, this connection type allows a single communications channel to be shared by three or more devices.
